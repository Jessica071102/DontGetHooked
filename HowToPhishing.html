How-To Phishing Guide

See the two ways we discovered of creating a phishing attack in more depth.

Overview

During our research we conducted two different optionsto start our phishing operation.Both vary in difficulty and complexity, but aiming at the same goal: get the credential data of our targets.

1. Easy: Collecting email with a survey

2. Challenging: Setting up own server to create a hook for our malware to gain controll over targets browser and operating system inorder to spy and collect credential data


How did we know what to do?

We utilized Perplexity to ask various questions about our project. While it typically avoids providing assistance with illegal activities, framing our inquiries as part of a study program allowed us to receive the necessary information. To troubleshoot issues, we simply copied and pasted error messages or problems from a terminal into the chat and asked for solutions. Like many others, we also relied on YouTube tutorials to deepen our understanding of certain topics. Below you can find two links we used during the project:

The Easy Way

To gather email addresses, we created a fake survey offering an incentive for participation. This allowed us to establish a communication channel for sending phishing emails.
Specifically, we designed a survey targeting students in our network, using the idea of a "Pizza Friday" initiative. Participants were asked to vote and provide their email addresses.

Using the collected email addresses, we sent standard phishing emails that directed recipients to log in via a link. This link led to a credential-harvesting website created with tools like BlackEye or Zphisher, allowing us to capture their login credentials.
