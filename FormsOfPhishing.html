Forms of Phishing

Explore the different ways cyber attackers target individuals

Email Phishing

Email phishing involves sending fraudulent emails to a broad audience that appear to come from trusted sources. These emails often include malicious links or attachments that steal your personal information.

Spear Phishing

Spear phishing is a targeted form of phishing where attackers tailor their messages to specific individuals or organizations, increasing the likelihood of success.

Smishing

Smishing uses text messages to trick victims into revealing personal information or clicking on malicious links.

Vishing

Vishing, or voice phishing, involves attackers calling victims and pretending to be trusted entities to extract sensitive information.

Whaling

A form of phishing targeting high-profile individuals, such as CEOs or executives, to steal sensitive company data.

Clone Phishing

Attackers clone a legitimate email or message and replace links or attachements with malicious versions.

Angler Phishing

Phishing attacks, which occur on social media platforms, such as fake customer service accounts, to trick users into sharing sensitive data.

Pop-up Phishing

Fake pop-up windows or ads that appear on websites, prompting users to enter personal details or download malware.
